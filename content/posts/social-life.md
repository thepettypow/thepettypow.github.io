# Fixing My Social Sh*t  

Alright, let’s get one thing straight—I’ve never been the “life of the party” type. Hell, I’ve barely been the “show up to the party” type. Social life? Yeah, that’s been a dumpster fire for most of my existence. And not the cool kind with marshmallows and stories around it. I’m talking awkward silences, overthinking every damn interaction, and bailing on plans like it’s my side hustle.  

### The Bad Stuff  
Let me break it down for you:  

- **Loneliness hits different.** Sure, being alone is fine—sometimes even necessary. But when it’s all the time? That shit *sucks*. You start to feel like a ghost watching everyone else live their lives.  
- **You’re your own worst hype man.** No squad means no one’s there to gas you up when you’re killing it or talk you down when you’re spiraling. It’s just you and your brain going 12 rounds in the ring. Spoiler: the brain wins.  
- **Networking is DOA.** Ever tried building a career or chasing a dream without knowing how to talk to people? Yeah, good luck with that. Turns out, “it’s not what you know, it’s who you know” isn’t just a cheesy line—it’s facts.  
- **You miss out.** On what? Everything. Friends, experiences, dumb stories you can laugh about later. Life’s not meant to be lived solo, man.  

### Why I’m Fixing It  
Look, I’m not saying I need to become some social butterfly who hugs strangers and knows every barista by name. But I gotta admit, this whole “loner life” thing isn’t cutting it anymore. Here’s why:  

1. **I deserve better.** Yeah, I said it. I deserve to have people in my corner. Friends, connections, whatever.  
2. **Growth needs roots.** You can’t grow if you’re floating around alone. I wanna be part of something—projects, ideas, communities.  
3. **Regret’s a bitch.** I don’t wanna look back and realize I missed out because I was too stuck in my head to reach out.  

### The Plan  
I’m not gonna bullshit you—this isn’t easy for me. Socializing feels like walking into a lion’s den wearing steak cologne. But I’m trying. Baby steps, ya know?  

- **Start small.** A text here, a coffee hang there. Nothing major.  
- **Push through the cringe.** Awkward? Yep. Worth it? Also yep.  
- **Be real.** No fake smiles, no pretending. Just me, showing up and hoping for the best.  

---

So yeah, that’s where I’m at. If you’re reading this and you’ve got your social life figured out, congrats—you’re officially a goddamn unicorn. For the rest of us? Let’s keep trying. Maybe we’ll figure this sh*t out together.
